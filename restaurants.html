Restaurant Reviews!

With over 24,000 places to eat in the Big Apple, deciding where to eat can be mind boggling. Check out a few of my latest reviews.

Ippudo

Ramen has been all the rage the past few years and this trend will not be going away anytime soon. Ippudo is a must if you are visiting the city. Be warned: the wait can be terribly long in the original location in East Village. Insider tip: Get there about an hour and a half early to put your name down, and then explore the uniqueness of that neighborhood while you wait and trust me the food is worth the wait. The most popular bowl is the Akumara ramen which comes in a silky broth and al dente thin noodles. This dining experience would not be complete without ordering the pork buns which melt in your mouth. Dont' eat pork? No problem, they also serve chicken buns which are just as tasty. Pizza!

A trip to New York would not be complete without some NY style thin crust pizza. With so many options to choose from your local neighborhood joints to iconic NY institutions, you won't have to travel too far to get a slice.
Locals and natives alike hit Bleeker Street Pizza. In fact, Food Network ranked this pizza joint as the best pizza in NYC!
Not too far is John's of Bleeker Street. They only sell by the pie not the slice so be sure to go with your friends and your appetite.
